# UK Compliance Checklists for Insurance Broker/MGA Call Centers

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2025-11-13
**Regulatory Framework:** FCA, ICOBS, IDD, GDPR, DISP, SM&CR
**Applies To:** Pikl Insurance (UK Ltd, Broker & MGA)

---

## Overview

This document provides comprehensive compliance checklists for different call types in UK insurance call centers operating as brokers and MGAs. These checklists are based on official UK regulatory requirements researched from FCA Handbook, GDPR/DPA 2018, IDD implementation, and SM&CR regulations.

---

## Universal Compliance Requirements (ALL CALL TYPES)

### 1. Call Opening Requirements

**Regulatory Source:** ICOBS, GDPR, SYSC 9

- [ ] **Firm Identification** (ICOBS 2.2.2R)
  - State company name clearly
  - Confirm regulatory status
  - _Example: "Good morning, this is [Name] calling from Pikl Insurance, we're authorized and regulated by the FCA"_

- [ ] **Call Recording Disclosure** (GDPR Article 13, SYSC 9)
  - Inform customer that call is being recorded
  - State purpose (quality assurance, compliance, training)
  - Mention retention period (5 years minimum)
  - _Example: "For quality assurance and regulatory purposes, this call is being recorded and will be retained for at least 5 years"_

- [ ] **GDPR Privacy Notice** (GDPR Articles 13/14)
  - Inform customer about data processing
  - State lawful basis (contract, legitimate interest)
  - Mention data rights (access, erasure, portability)
  - _Example: "We process your personal data to provide insurance services. You have the right to access and request deletion of your data. For more information, please see our privacy policy"_

### 2. Data Protection & Verification

**Regulatory Source:** GDPR, DPA 2018, ICOBS

- [ ] **DPA Verification Before Access** (GDPR Article 32, ICOBS 2.4)
  - Verify customer identity BEFORE accessing policy details
  - Collect: Full name + Date of Birth (minimum)
  - Additional verification for sensitive changes (policy number, address, postcode)
  - _Example: "Before I can access your policy, I need to verify your identity. Can you please confirm your full name and date of birth?"_

- [ ] **Lawful Basis for Processing** (GDPR Article 6)
  - Confirm lawful basis for processing data
  - Usually: Contract performance or Legitimate interest
  - Document basis in call notes

- [ ] **Data Security** (GDPR Article 32)
  - Do not read full card numbers aloud
  - Do not store card CVV codes
  - Use secure payment systems (PCI DSS compliant)
  - Confirm secure method for sensitive data transmission

### 3. Treating Customers Fairly (TCF)

**Regulatory Source:** FCA PRIN 6, ICOBS

- [ ] **TCF Outcome 1: Fair Treatment Throughout**
  - No pressure selling or aggressive tactics
  - Allow customer time to make decisions
  - Respect customer's right to decline

- [ ] **TCF Outcome 2: Clear, Fair, Not Misleading Information**
  - Explain products in plain language
  - Avoid jargon without explanation
  - Correct any misunderstandings promptly

- [ ] **TCF Outcome 3: Product Suitability**
  - Understand customer needs before recommending
  - Only recommend suitable products
  - Explain why product meets their needs

- [ ] **TCF Outcome 4: Product Performance**
  - Clearly explain what is and isn't covered
  - Mention any significant exclusions
  - Set realistic expectations

- [ ] **TCF Outcome 5: No Unreasonable Barriers**
  - Make it easy for customer to switch, cancel, or claim
  - Explain processes clearly
  - Provide contact information for further assistance

- [ ] **TCF Outcome 6: No Unreasonable Post-Sale Barriers**
  - Ensure customer understands next steps
  - Provide clear contact methods
  - Explain how to make claims or changes

### 4. Mandatory Disclosures

**Regulatory Source:** ICOBS, IDD

- [ ] **Service Type** (ICOBS 4.2)
  - State whether providing advice or information only
  - Explain difference between advised and non-advised sales
  - _Advised: "Based on your circumstances, I recommend..."_
  - _Non-advised: "I can provide information to help you decide, but I'm not providing advice"_

- [ ] **Remuneration** (ICOBS 4.3, IDD Article 19)
  - Disclose if paid by commission or fee
  - State if customer requests specific details
  - _Example: "We receive commission from insurers for policies sold"_

- [ ] **Complaints Procedure** (DISP 1.2)
  - Inform customer of right to complain
  - Provide complaints contact details
  - Mention Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS)
  - _Example: "If you're unhappy with our service, you can complain by calling [number] or emailing [email]. If we can't resolve it, you can refer to the Financial Ombudsman Service"_

### 5. Vulnerable Customers

**Regulatory Source:** FCA Guidance on Vulnerable Customers (FG21/1)

- [ ] **Identify Vulnerability Indicators**
  - Health issues, mental capacity concerns
  - Financial difficulties, debt
  - Life events (bereavement, divorce, job loss)
  - Language barriers, low literacy

- [ ] **Adjust Communication**
  - Use simpler language if needed
  - Provide extra time for decisions
  - Offer to send written confirmation
  - Suggest support person can be present

- [ ] **Document Vulnerability**
  - Record vulnerability in CRM
  - Flag for future interactions
  - Follow internal vulnerable customer procedures

- [ ] **Additional Support**
  - Offer callback at better time
  - Provide alternative communication methods
  - Refer to specialist support teams if needed

### 6. Call Closing Requirements

**Regulatory Source:** ICOBS, TCF

- [ ] **Summary of Discussion**
  - Recap key points covered
  - Confirm customer understanding
  - Address any remaining questions

- [ ] **Next Steps**
  - Clearly explain what happens next
  - Provide timelines for actions
  - Confirm any customer actions required

- [ ] **Documentation**
  - Confirm details will be sent by email/post
  - Provide reference number for follow-up
  - Give direct contact information

- [ ] **Thank & Close**
  - Thank customer for their time
  - Offer further assistance if needed
  - Professional sign-off

## Call Type 1: NEW BUSINESS SALES CALL

**Applies to:** Customer purchasing new insurance policy

### Pre-Sale Requirements

**Regulatory Source:** ICOBS 6.1, IDD Articles 18-20

- [ ] **Needs Assessment** (ICOBS 6.1A, IDD Article 20)
  - Ask questions about customer circumstances
  - Understand property/asset being insured
  - Identify specific risks and requirements
  - Document customer needs in CRM

- [ ] **Fair Value Assessment** (Consumer Duty)
  - Consider if product provides fair value
  - Explain benefits relative to price
  - Mention alternative options if appropriate

- [ ] **Target Market Confirmation** (IDD Article 25, Product Governance)
  - Confirm customer fits product target market
  - Document why product is suitable
  - If outside target market, escalate or decline

### Product Information & Disclosures

**Regulatory Source:** ICOBS 6.1, IDD Article 20

- [ ] **Product Information Document (PID)** (IDD Article 20)
  - Confirm customer will receive/has received PID
  - Highlight key features, benefits, and exclusions
  - Explain main risks covered
  - _Example: "I'll email you the Product Information Document which summarizes the key features, benefits, and exclusions"_

- [ ] **Insurance Product Information Document (IPID)** (IDD)
  - Provide standardized IPID before contract conclusion
  - Explain format and purpose
  - Allow time for customer to review

- [ ] **Policy Terms & Conditions**
  - Confirm customer will receive full policy wording
  - Highlight any significant exclusions or limitations
  - Explain endorsements or additional covers

- [ ] **Price Breakdown** (ICOBS 6.1.5R)
  - State total premium amount clearly
  - Break down: premium, IPT, admin fees, optional extras
  - Explain payment options (annual, monthly)
  - Mention any interest charges for instalments
  - _Example: "The total annual premium is £450, which includes £50 Insurance Premium Tax and a £25 admin fee. You can pay monthly for £40 per month with a small interest charge"_

### Suitability & Advice (ADVISED SALES ONLY)

**Regulatory Source:** ICOBS 4.6, 4.7, IDD Article 20

- [ ] **Suitability Assessment** (ICOBS 4.6A)
  - Conduct thorough needs analysis
  - Consider customer demands and needs
  - Match product features to requirements
  - Document suitability reasoning

- [ ] **Personal Recommendation** (ICOBS 4.7)
  - Clearly state "I recommend this product for you because..."
  - Explain why it meets their specific needs
  - Mention why alternatives were not recommended

- [ ] **Suitability Statement** (ICOBS 4.7.1R)
  - Provide written suitability statement
  - Explain reasons for recommendation
  - Include customer needs and how product meets them

### Cooling-Off Rights

**Regulatory Source:** ICOBS 7.1

- [ ] **14-Day Cooling-Off Period** (ICOBS 7.1.4R)
  - Inform customer of 14-day cancellation right
  - Explain how to cancel (phone, email, writing)
  - Mention any refund terms (pro-rata, admin fees)
  - _Example: "You have 14 days from the start date to cancel for a full refund if you change your mind"_

- [ ] **Cancellation After 14 Days**
  - Explain cancellation rights after cooling-off
  - Mention any short-period cancellation charges
  - State refund calculation method

### Payment & Contract Conclusion

**Regulatory Source:** ICOBS, Payment Services Regulations

- [ ] **Payment Details**
  - Confirm payment method (card, direct debit)
  - Take card details securely (PCI DSS)
  - Never store CVV code
  - Confirm payment authorization

- [ ] **Contract Start Date**
  - Clearly state when cover begins
  - Confirm start date/time
  - Explain any waiting periods or excesses

- [ ] **Documentation Confirmation**
  - Confirm policy documents will be sent
  - State delivery method (email, post)
  - Provide timeframe for receipt
  - Give policy number for reference

### Complaint Triggers (Monitor For)

**Regulatory Source:** DISP

- Customer expresses dissatisfaction with service
- Customer disputes information provided
- Customer mentions previous complaints
- Customer requests to speak to manager about issue
- Customer mentions solicitor or ombudsman

## Call Type 2: RENEWALS CALL

**Applies to:** Existing customer renewing policy

### Pre-Renewal Requirements

**Regulatory Source:** ICOBS 6.1.4C, Consumer Duty

- [ ] **Renewal Invite Sent** (ICOBS 6.1.4C)
  - Confirm customer received renewal invite 21+ days before
  - Mention new premium and any changes
  - Explain reason for premium increase if applicable

- [ ] **Fair Value at Renewal** (Consumer Duty)
  - Ensure renewal price offers fair value
  - Avoid "price walking" (excessive loyalty penalties)
  - Explain price changes transparently

- [ ] **Needs Review** (Good Practice)
  - Ask if customer circumstances have changed
  - Verify property/asset details still accurate
  - Update any changes to risk

### Material Changes

**Regulatory Source:** ICOBS 6.1.5R, IDD

- [ ] **Disclosure of Changes** (ICOBS 6.1.5R)
  - Highlight any changes to cover
  - Explain changes to terms and conditions
  - Mention changes to excess or deductibles
  - Confirm changes to premium breakdown

- [ ] **Customer Acknowledgment**
  - Ensure customer understands changes
  - Allow opportunity for questions
  - Confirm customer acceptance of changes

### Renewal Confirmation

**Regulatory Source:** ICOBS

- [ ] **Auto-Renewal Disclosure** (ICOBS 6.1.4C)
  - If auto-renewing, confirm customer understands
  - Explain how to opt out or cancel
  - State date by which they must cancel to avoid charge

- [ ] **Payment Confirmation**
  - Confirm payment method (existing DD, new payment)
  - Verify direct debit details if changed
  - Confirm payment date

- [ ] **Renewal Documentation**
  - Confirm customer will receive renewal documents
  - Include new policy schedule, updated terms
  - Provide renewal policy number

### Retention & Alternative Offers

**Regulatory Source:** TCF, Consumer Duty

- [ ] **Fair Treatment in Retention** (TCF)
  - Do not pressure customer to renew
  - Present alternative options if available
  - Allow customer to decline renewal

- [ ] **Alternative Products**
  - Offer alternative products if current doesn't suit
  - Explain benefits of alternatives
  - Respect customer choice to switch or cancel

## Call Type 3: MID-TERM ADJUSTMENT (MTA) CALL

**Applies to:** Changes to existing policy during policy term

### Change Request Verification

**Regulatory Source:** ICOBS, GDPR

- [ ] **DPA Verification** (GDPR, ICOBS 2.4)
  - Verify customer identity before making changes
  - Use enhanced verification for significant changes
  - Confirm authority to make changes (policyholder/authorized user)

- [ ] **Change Details**
  - Clearly document requested change
  - Confirm details with customer
  - Explain impact on cover

### Risk Assessment & Pricing

**Regulatory Source:** ICOBS 6.1.5R

- [ ] **Risk Impact** (ICOBS 6.1.5R)
  - Explain how change affects risk
  - State if premium increases or decreases
  - Mention any new exclusions or endorsements

- [ ] **Premium Adjustment**
  - Clearly state additional premium or refund
  - Break down calculation (pro-rata, admin fees)
  - Explain payment method for additional premium
  - _Example: "Your premium will increase by £25 for the remaining 6 months. The breakdown is £20 additional premium and a £5 admin fee"_

### Mid-Term Cancellation (If Applicable)

**Regulatory Source:** ICOBS 7.1

- [ ] **Cancellation Rights** (ICOBS 7.1)
  - Explain cancellation terms
  - Mention any cancellation fees
  - Calculate refund (pro-rata, short-period scale)

- [ ] **Refund Calculation**
  - State refund amount clearly
  - Explain calculation method
  - Confirm refund timeframe and method

### Change Confirmation

**Regulatory Source:** ICOBS

- [ ] **Change Summary**
  - Recap all changes made
  - Confirm new cover details
  - State effective date of changes

- [ ] **Documentation**
  - Confirm MTA endorsement will be sent
  - Include updated policy schedule
  - Provide MTA reference number

## Call Type 4: CLAIMS INQUIRY/FNOL (First Notification of Loss)

**Applies to:** Customer calling about a claim

### Claims Handling Requirements

**Regulatory Source:** ICOBS 8, FCA Claims Handling Rules

- [ ] **Claims Reporting** (ICOBS 8.1)
  - Take full details of loss/incident
  - Record date, time, location
  - Understand nature of claim
  - Document circumstances

- [ ] **Empathy & Support** (TCF)
  - Show empathy for customer situation
  - Provide reassurance and support
  - Explain process clearly
  - Avoid jargon

### Information Gathering

**Regulatory Source:** ICOBS 8

- [ ] **Incident Details**
  - Date and time of incident
  - Location and circumstances
  - Type of damage or loss
  - Any third parties involved

- [ ] **Emergency Actions**
  - Advise on immediate steps (safety, security)
  - Arrange emergency repairs if needed
  - Provide emergency contact numbers

- [ ] **Documentation Requirements**
  - Explain evidence needed (photos, receipts, reports)
  - Set expectations for claim process
  - Provide claim reference number

### Claims Process Explanation

**Regulatory Source:** ICOBS 8, TCF

- [ ] **Claims Process** (ICOBS 8.1)
  - Explain next steps in claims process
  - State timelines for assessment
  - Mention claims handler contact details
  - Explain potential outcomes

- [ ] **Policy Coverage** (ICOBS 8.1)
  - Confirm cover applies (if possible at this stage)
  - Explain any excesses or deductibles
  - Mention any claim limits or conditions
  - If claim may not be covered, explain why

### Fair Claims Handling

**Regulatory Source:** FCA Principle 6 (TCF), ICOBS 8

- [ ] **Fair & Prompt Handling** (TCF, ICOBS 8.1.1R)
  - Handle claim promptly and fairly
  - Do not unreasonably reject claims
  - Explain reasons if claim denied
  - Provide clear process for dispute

- [ ] **Settlement Communication**
  - Explain settlement process
  - State how payment will be made
  - Confirm timelines for resolution

### Complaint Detection

**Regulatory Source:** DISP

- [ ] **Monitor for Dissatisfaction**
  - Customer unhappy with claim decision
  - Customer disputes claim handling
  - Customer mentions delays or poor service
  - Log as complaint if criteria met

## Call Type 5: COMPLAINTS CALL

**Applies to:** Customer making a formal complaint

### Complaint Identification

**Regulatory Source:** DISP 1.2, 1.4

- [ ] **Recognize Complaint** (DISP 1.4)
  - Expression of dissatisfaction
  - About firm's provision of services
  - Actual or potential financial loss, material distress, material inconvenience
  - If uncertain, treat as complaint (better safe than sorry)

- [ ] **Do NOT Discourage** (TCF, DISP 1.2)
  - Never discourage customer from complaining
  - Do not minimize concerns
  - Take complaint seriously

### Complaint Handling Process

**Regulatory Source:** DISP 1

- [ ] **Acknowledge Immediately** (DISP 1.5)
  - Acknowledge complaint on call
  - Express regret for customer's experience (NOT admission of liability)
  - Thank customer for raising issue
  - _Example: "I'm sorry to hear about your experience. Thank you for bringing this to our attention. I'm logging this as a formal complaint"_

- [ ] **Log Complaint** (DISP 1.3)
  - Record complaint in CRM immediately
  - Assign complaint reference number
  - Document full details of issue
  - Categorize by type and severity

- [ ] **Provide Reference Number** (DISP 1.5)
  - Give customer complaint reference number
  - Confirm customer has noted it down
  - Explain they can quote this for follow-up

### Investigation & Resolution Timeline

**Regulatory Source:** DISP 1.6

- [ ] **Explain Process** (DISP 1.6)
  - State complaint will be investigated
  - Mention who will handle it (complaints team, manager)
  - Explain process and timeline
  - _Example: "Your complaint will be investigated by our complaints team within 5 business days. They'll call or email you with updates"_

- [ ] **Timeline Expectations** (DISP 1.6)
  - 3-day response: Acknowledge and provide update or resolution
  - 8-week maximum: Must provide final response or explain delay
  - State Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS) rights if not resolved in 8 weeks

- [ ] **Contact Information** (DISP 1.6)
  - Provide complaints team contact details
  - Confirm method customer prefers (phone, email, post)
  - State when they can expect to hear back

### Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS) Rights

**Regulatory Source:** DISP 1.9

- [ ] **FOS Information** (DISP 1.9)
  - Inform customer of right to refer to FOS
  - State when they can refer (after 8 weeks or if dissatisfied with final response)
  - Provide FOS contact details (if at final response stage)
  - Confirm FOS service is free to customer
  - _FOS Details: financial-ombudsman.org.uk, 0800 023 4567_

### Immediate Resolution (If Possible)

**Regulatory Source:** DISP 1.6

- [ ] **Attempt Resolution** (Good Practice)
  - If issue can be resolved immediately, do so
  - Offer appropriate remedy (refund, goodwill gesture, correction)
  - Confirm customer is satisfied with resolution
  - Still log as complaint and document resolution

- [ ] **Close Complaint** (DISP 1.6)
  - Confirm complaint is closed
  - Provide written confirmation of resolution
  - Confirm customer has no further concerns

## Call Type 6: CUSTOMER SERVICE / GENERAL INQUIRY

**Applies to:** General questions, policy servicing, information requests

### Information Requests

**Regulatory Source:** GDPR, ICOBS

- [ ] **DPA Verification** (GDPR, ICOBS 2.4)
  - Verify identity before providing policy information
  - Use standard verification (name, DOB)
  - Do not disclose sensitive information without verification

- [ ] **Provide Accurate Information** (TCF, ICOBS)
  - Answer questions accurately and completely
  - If unsure, confirm you'll find out and call back
  - Do not guess or provide incorrect information

- [ ] **Documentation** (Good Practice)
  - Offer to send information in writing if needed
  - Confirm email address or postal address
  - Provide reference for follow-up

### Policy Servicing

**Regulatory Source:** TCF, Consumer Duty

- [ ] **Customer-Friendly Service** (TCF, Consumer Duty)
  - Make it easy for customer to service policy
  - Explain processes clearly
  - Provide timelines for actions
  - Remove unnecessary barriers

- [ ] **Document Interaction**
  - Log all servicing requests in CRM
  - Record actions taken
  - Set follow-up tasks if needed

### Sales Opportunities (If Applicable)

**Regulatory Source:** ICOBS, TCF

- [ ] **Identify Needs** (Good Practice)
  - Listen for customer needs or gaps in cover
  - Mention relevant products if appropriate
  - Do not hard sell or pressure

- [ ] **Separate Advice** (ICOBS 4.6)
  - If providing advice, follow advised sales process
  - Clearly state you are now providing advice
  - Conduct needs assessment

## Compliance Monitoring & Scoring

### How to Use These Checklists in QA

1. **Call Type Identification**
   - Determine primary call type from transcript
   - Apply relevant checklist for that call type
   - Also apply universal requirements (applies to all calls)

2. **Checklist Completion**
   - Mark each item as: ✅ Compliant, ❌ Non-Compliant, ⚠️ Partial, N/A Not Applicable
   - Note specific evidence from transcript (quote, timestamp)
   - Categorize by severity: Critical, High, Medium, Low

3. **Compliance Scoring**
   - Critical items: Must be 100% compliant (FCA Principle 6, GDPR, DISP)
   - High priority: Should be 90%+ compliant (TCF, Disclosures, Data Protection)
   - Medium priority: Target 80%+ (Best practices, Product knowledge)
   - Low priority: Target 70%+ (General quality standards)

4. **Compliance Flags**
   - **Critical Flag**: Missing call recording disclosure, DPA breach, misleading information, pressure selling
   - **High Flag**: Missing complaints procedure disclosure, inadequate needs assessment, unclear pricing
   - **Medium Flag**: Minor disclosure omissions, procedural gaps
   - **Low Flag**: Quality improvements, coaching opportunities

5. **Reporting**
   - Provide checklist completion rate per call
   - Highlight critical and high-priority non-compliance
   - Generate coaching recommendations for gaps
   - Escalate critical compliance breaches immediately

## Severity Levels for Non-Compliance

### Critical (Immediate Escalation Required)

**Regulatory Risk:** High - Regulatory breach, customer harm, legal liability

- Missing call recording disclosure
- Data protection breach (accessing data without DPA verification)
- Providing misleading or false information
- Pressure selling or unfair treatment
- Ignoring or discouraging complaints
- Non-compliance with SM&CR conduct rules

**Action:** Immediate escalation to compliance team, coaching required, possible disciplinary action

### High (Priority Remediation)

**Regulatory Risk:** Medium-High - Potential regulatory breach, customer detriment

- Missing mandatory disclosures (firm identity, remuneration, complaints)
- Inadequate needs assessment for advised sales
- No cooling-off rights explained
- Missing GDPR privacy notice
- Poor complaint handling
- No suitability statement for advised sales

**Action:** Coaching required within 5 days, follow-up call review within 30 days

### Medium (Coaching Opportunity)

**Regulatory Risk:** Low-Medium - Procedural gap, quality concern

- Incomplete needs discovery
- Unclear pricing breakdown
- Missing follow-up confirmation
- Insufficient vulnerable customer identification
- Documentation gaps

**Action:** Coaching recommended, monitoring for improvement

### Low (Quality Improvement)

**Regulatory Risk:** Low - Best practice opportunity

- Limited empathy or rapport
- Could explain product features more clearly
- Basic professionalism improvements
- Documentation could be more detailed

**Action:** Include in routine feedback, monitor trends

## Integration with QA Assist System

### Recommended Implementation

1. **Expand Compliance Dimension**
   - Split current generic "compliance" score into sub-dimensions:
     - Mandatory Disclosures (0-10)
     - Data Protection & GDPR (0-10)
     - TCF Compliance (0-10)
     - Complaints Handling (0-10, or N/A)
     - Sales Process Compliance (0-10, for sales calls)

2. **Add Checklist Validation**
   - Automatically check transcript for:
     - Call recording disclosure phrases
     - DPA verification patterns
     - Mandatory disclosure keywords
     - Complaints trigger phrases
   - Flag missing items for manual review

3. **Severity-Based Scoring**
   - Critical non-compliance = Auto-fail (score 0)
   - High priority gaps = Significant score reduction
   - Medium/low gaps = Minor score reduction

4. **Call Type Detection**
   - Use AI to detect call type from transcript
   - Apply relevant checklist automatically
   - Adjust scoring based on call type requirements

5. **Compliance Reporting**
   - Generate compliance summary per call
   - Highlight critical flags for immediate escalation
   - Provide coaching recommendations based on gaps
   - Track compliance trends over time
